# Puri Jagannath Temple - Research

## Historical Background
- **Construction**: The temple was built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva of the Ganga Dynasty.
- **Mythological Origins**: According to legend, the temple was originally built by King Indradyumna, who was guided by Lord Vishnu to build a temple for Lord Jagannath.
- **Spiritual Significance**: It is one of the four Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus.

## Deities and Temple Complex
- **Main Deities**: The temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of Lord Vishnu, along with his elder brother Balabhadra and sister Subhadra. The idols are made of sacred neem wood (Daru).
- **Temple Architecture**: The temple is built in the Kalinga architectural style and has a towering spire (Vimana) that is 65 meters high. The complex is enclosed by two concentric walls, the Kuruma Bheda and the Meghnad Pachira.
- **Singhadwara**: The main entrance to the temple is on the eastern side and is known as the Singhadwara, or the Lion Gate.
- **Nilachakra**: A blue wheel made of an alloy of eight metals (asta dhatu) is perched on top of the temple's spire. It is believed that seeing the Nilachakra is as auspicious as seeing the Lord himself.
- **Yama Shila**: A stone that is believed to absolve one of their sins if they step on it. However, the travelogue mentions a belief that stepping on it removes the benefits of the darshan. This needs to be clarified.

## Maha Prasad
- **Significance**: The temple has one of the largest kitchens in the world, where food is cooked in traditional earthen pots and offered to Lord Jagannath. This offering, known as Maha Prasad, is then distributed to the devotees.
- **Ananda Bazaar**: A bustling marketplace within the temple complex where the Maha Prasad is sold.

## Visitor Information
- **Location**: Puri, Odisha.
- **How to Reach**:
  - **Airport**: Biju Patnaik International Airport (BBI) in Bhubaneswar, approximately 60 km away.
  - **Railway**: Puri is a major railway junction.
- **Timings**: 5:00 AM to 12:00 AM.
- **Entry**: Only Hindus are allowed inside the temple.
- **Festivals**:
  - **Rath Yatra**: The most famous festival, where the deities are taken out in grand chariots.
  - **Snana Yatra**: A ceremonial bathing festival of the deities.
  - **Chandan Yatra**: A 21-day festival.
- **Accessibility**: The temple provides facilities for senior citizens and differently-abled devotees.
