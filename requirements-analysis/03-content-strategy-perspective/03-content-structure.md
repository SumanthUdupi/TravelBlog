# Content Structure & Information Architecture

## Summary
This detailed information architecture blueprint defines the hierarchy, models, and templates for content on the Odisha Sacred Sites Travel Blog. It ensures discoverability, maintainability, and scalability while embracing a rustic, spiritual aesthetic with scholarly yet accessible writing focused on Odisha's sacred sites.

## Information Architecture Overview
The IA is organized around a user-centric journey: Discovery → Exploration → Immersion → Reflection. Content is structured to guide pilgrims and travelers through Odisha's sacred landscape, emphasizing spiritual depth and cultural richness.

### Core Principles
- **Spiritual Depth**: Content prioritizes meaningful connections to sacred sites.
- **Accessibility**: Scholarly insights made approachable for all users.
- **Rustic Aesthetic**: Simple, organic navigation reflecting Odisha's natural beauty.
- **Odisha Focus**: Centered on temples, festivals, and cultural heritage.

## Hierarchy of Content Elements

### Navigation Menus
- **Primary Navigation**: Home, Temples, Blogs, Immersive Experiences, About
- **Secondary Navigation**: Sub-menus for regions (e.g., Puri, Bhubaneswar), types (e.g., Shakti Peethas, Sun Temples)
- **Footer Navigation**: Contact, Privacy, Site Map, Social Links

### Page Layouts
- **Header**: Logo, navigation, search bar
- **Main Content Area**: Hero section, content blocks, sidebar for related content
- **Footer**: Newsletter signup, quick links, copyright

### Blog Post Formats
- **Standard Post**: Title, byline, featured image, body with headings, multimedia, tags
- **Gallery Post**: Image-heavy with captions and light text
- **Video/Audio Post**: Embedded media with transcript
- **Series Post**: Part of a pilgrimage route series with navigation to next/previous

## Content Models
- **Article**: title, summary, hero image, body, tags, published date, source references
- **Place/Event**: location, visiting hours, travel tips, images, map, accessibility details
- **Collection**: curated group of articles by theme (e.g., pilgrimage routes)
- **Immersive Experience**: interactive elements, narratives, multimedia

## Taxonomy & Tagging
- Suggested top-level categories: Places, Guides, Stories, Collections, Research
- Tags for facets: region, temple type, festival, accessibility, difficulty

## Page Templates

### Landing Page
- Hero section with inspirational image of Odisha landscape
- Featured temples and routes
- Call-to-action for immersive experiences

### Detail Page (Temple)
- Overview: Historical context, spiritual significance
- Visiting Information: Hours, rituals, accessibility
- Multimedia Gallery: Images, videos, audio chants
- Related Content: Similar temples, blog posts

### Blog Page
- Post list with filters by category, date
- Search functionality
- Pagination

### Immersive Experience Page
- Narrative introduction
- Interactive map or timeline
- Multimedia sections: 360° views, audio stories
- Reflection prompts

### Collection Page
- Curated list with descriptions
- Map view
- Sorting and filtering options

## Navigation & Discovery
- Add faceted search (region, accessibility, type)
- Surface related content by semantic tags and shared motifs (e.g., deity, era)

## User Flow and Content Organization

### Wireframe-like Descriptions

**Home Page Flow:**
- User lands on hero image → Scrolls to featured temples → Clicks on a temple → Detail page

**Temple Exploration Flow:**
- Detail page: Read overview → View gallery → Access related blogs → Book visit or share

**Blog Discovery:**
- Blog index → Filter by region → Read post → Navigate to related temple

**Immersive Journey:**
- Select experience → Follow narrative → Interact with media → Complete reflection

### Content Organization
- Hierarchical: Categories > Subcategories > Individual pages
- Cross-linked: Internal links between temples, blogs, experiences

## Readability and User Experience Strategies

### Headings and Structure
- Use H1 for page title, H2 for sections, H3 for subsections
- Keep headings descriptive and spiritual (e.g., "The Divine Presence at Konark")

### Lists and Bullet Points
- Bullet lists for features, tips
- Numbered lists for steps in rituals or travel guides

### Multimedia Integration
- Images: High-quality, culturally sensitive photos with alt text
- Videos: Short clips of rituals, narrated by locals
- Audio: Chants, stories in Odia and English
- Interactive: Maps, timelines for engagement

### Accessibility Enhancements
- Alt text for images
- Transcripts for audio/video
- High contrast for readability
